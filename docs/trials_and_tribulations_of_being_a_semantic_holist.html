One of the chief joys of philosophy is the license it gives for largely unsuccessful attempts to foist one's opinions on more or less innocent passers by. The field is thus apt to attract those who enjoy the the painless suffering of being a lone voice in the wilderness. It would, therefore, be disingenuous to count the fact that semantic_holism and the accompanying doctrine of indeterminacy of translation are widely held to be either false, self-contradictory or incoherent as a tribulation for sincere believers . What does gall somewhat, however, is that the very tools which its critics use to formulate their misguided objections are themselves beautiful exemplars of semantic holism's truth.
