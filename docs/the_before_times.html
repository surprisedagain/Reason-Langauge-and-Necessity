The times before the enlightenment when the world was regarded (by many influential European (and at least some Chinese) philosophers) as permeated by reason. That the properties instantiated by external objects were the same as/similar to/akin to the concepts in our minds. (e.g. The color in an object is like the sensation of that color in our mind.) AND That knowledge (at least in its best examples) is justified by deduction from initial principles known with certainty.
