assumptions Confirmation is a relationship between at least two entities. on one side of this relationship are the entities that are confirmed, on the other side are the entities that confirm them.

Although there are at least two entities involved in a confirmation relationship, at this preliminary stage we should not assume that it is just two entities that are thus related. It may be that an entity is confirmed only relative to, say, background assumptions, or prior beliefs, in which case those background assumptions. It may also be the case that these entities are only confirmed to a degree and that degree, if any, would also be involved in the relationship of confirmation. In any case confirmation is a relationship between (at least) the things that are confirmed and the things that confirm them.

The things that are confirmed are representations, things that can be true or false, for if an entity is confirmed then we are justified in believing it to be true (to whatever degree, and relative to whatever background assumptions or other relata it is confirmed).
